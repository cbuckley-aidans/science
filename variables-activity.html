How to Find the Variables in a Research Question Hide
1

Find the Independent Variable

The independent variable is the thing you are deliberately changing or controlling. You decide what it will be before the experiment begins.

Ask yourself: "What am I changing or setting up in this experiment?"

I control it → Independent → X-Axis (across the bottom)
2

Find the Dependent Variable

The dependent variable is what you measure or observe during the experiment. You do not know what it will be until you run the experiment. It depends on the independent variable.

Ask yourself: "What am I measuring or recording as a result?"

It depends on the other one → Dependent → Y-Axis (up the side)

Quick memory trick: Dependent goes Down the side of the graph (the Y-axis runs vertically).

Worked Example

Use the two questions above to work through this example before you practise.

Research Question

"Does a candle burn down faster when it is placed in a smaller container?"

Step 1 (what am I changing?): The size of the container. That is the independent variable.

Step 2 (what am I measuring?): How fast the candle burns down. That is the dependent variable.

Key Takeaways
Remember These Rules Independent Variable: What you change or control. Goes on the X-axis. Ask: "What am I changing?"

Dependent Variable: What you measure in response. Goes on the Y-axis. Ask: "What am I recording?" Remember: Dependent starts with D, and so does Down the side of the graph.
