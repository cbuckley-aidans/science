Using mass and volume to understand how matter is packed together

Mass
Mass is the amount of matter in an object.
It is measured using a digital scale and recorded in grams (g).
Let’s recall: Volume
On your whiteboard — what is volume and how do we calculate it for a rectangular prism?
30 seconds thinking time, then chin your boards.
Volume
Volume is the amount of space an object occupies.
It is measured in cm³.
Formula
Volume = Length × Width × Height
Key Concept
What is Density?
Definition
Density is how much mass is packed into a given volume. It tells us how tightly matter is squished together.
Unit
Density is measured in grams per cubic centimetre (g/cm³).
Think of it this way: a brick and a sponge can be the same size — but the brick has much more mass packed in. The brick is denser.
The Formula
Density = Mass ÷ Volume
g/cm³  =  g  ÷  cm³
Three steps to find density
1
Measure Mass
Place the object on a digital scale. Record the mass in grams (g).
2
Calculate Volume
Measure length, width, and height in cm. Multiply them together: L × W × H
3
Calculate Density
Divide mass by volume: Density = Mass ÷ Volume. Record your answer in g/cm³.

Extending the Concept
Float or Sink?
The Rule
Water has a density of 1 g/cm³.
Density > 1 g/cm³
Sink
Denser than water — it goes down.
↔
Density < 1 g/cm³
Float
Less dense than water — it rises.
On your whiteboard: look back at our three practice problems. Which objects would float? Which would sink? Write down the density and your prediction for each.
Answers
